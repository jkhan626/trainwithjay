## PART 1: CLOSING YOUR FIRST CLIENTS (Weeks 1-4)

### The First Consultation — 7-Step Script
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes in person (perfect for in-home assessment)

1. **Welcome & Rapport (5 min)** — "Thanks for having me, excited to chat about what you're trying to achieve"
2. **Background & Health (5 min)** — Ask about injury history, medications, surgeries (use PAR-Q form)
3. **Goals & Motivation (5 min)** — "What's your main goal and why is it important now?" (Ask twice to find the real reason)
4. **Lifestyle Assessment (5 min)** — Work schedule, sleep, stress, nutrition, how many days they can realistically train
5. **Your Role Explanation (5 min)** — Show them what you do: programming, form coaching, progression, accountability
6. **Program Recommendation (15 min)** — Present ONE recommendation with rationale: "Based on X, I think Y is best because Z"
7. **Handle Questions & Close (10 min)** — Specific follow-up timing: "I'll text Thursday to check in" (not vague)

**Critical Talking Ratio:** You talk 30%, they talk 70%. Shut up and listen.

**Documents to Send BEFORE Consultation:**
- Goals questionnaire
- Health history/PAR-Q form
- Lifestyle assessment
- Informed consent

**Most Important:** Don't quote price until AFTER you've established value. Price comes last.

---

### Handling the 5 Most Common Objections

**OBJECTION 1: "It's too expensive"**
- Don't discount immediately (kills credibility)
- Script: "Can I ask, is it the total amount or the monthly commitment?" Then: "What would it be worth to resolve this in 12 weeks?"
- Root cause: Value hasn't been clearly communicated, not inability to afford

**OBJECTION 2: "I need to think about it"**
- Root cause: Unvoiced concern (price, commitment, or doubt)
- Script: "What specifically are you weighing up? I'd rather ensure you have all the info"
- This objection loses the most sales when trainers accept it passively

**OBJECTION 3: "I don't have the time"**
- Script: "What does your typical week look like? Let's find a realistic slot" OR "What would need to be true for this to become a priority?"
- Could be genuine scheduling or lack of prioritization

**OBJECTION 4: "I need to ask my partner"**
- Script: "That makes sense. Is there anything I can share with them, or would a brief call with you both be helpful?"
- Don't assume deflection; treat as genuine

**OBJECTION 5: "I'll start next month"**
- Script: "What's happening next month that makes it better than now?" Then: "What would a 30-day delay mean for your goal?"
- No urgency has been established

**Follow-up Cadence:**
- First follow-up: 2-3 days after consultation
- Second follow-up: One week later if no response
- Final check-in: 2-3 weeks after initial consultation
- Stop after three follow-ups with no engagement

### Pricing Script (For Pre-Consultation Inquiry)
When someone asks about price before consulting:

**Script:** "My programmes run from $75 to (your future rate) per session depending on what you need. The consultation helps me work out exactly what would serve you best."

**Key Numbers (from Encyclopedia):**
- US in-person personal training range: **$60–$120/session**
- Your $75 rate: Lower-mid of range, room to grow toward $90
- Your future rate (at 70% booked): $75–90
- When to raise: When demand exceeds supply, typically 12–24 months in

**Pricing Increase Communication:**
When you do raise (to $75–90 later):
- Minimum notice: 4-6 weeks
- Tone: "Be direct and unapologetic. A price increase does not require extensive justification"
- Frame it: "Investment in continued education and better programming" (not "cost of living")
- Long-term clients: Often accept gracefully; consider half increase now, rest in 6 months

## PART 2: ONBOARDING (Days 1-30) — Retention Starts Here

### Day 1 Checklist (Immediate)
- [ ] Send service agreement + payment details
- [ ] Send PAR-Q + lifestyle questionnaire
- [ ] Send welcome pack (your philosophy, expectations, logistics)
- [ ] Book first session (specific date/time, not open-ended)

### Pre-Session Email (24-48 hours before)
"Hey [name], looking forward to training together tomorrow at [time]. What to bring: comfortable clothes, water, and dumbbells if you have them (I'm bringing bands/mat). See you then!"

### First Session Components
- Recap goals with any new info
- Movement screening/baseline assessment (note posture, range of motion, mobility gaps)
- Show your training style (not extreme, but intentional)
- Leave them energized and confident
- Recap what's coming next week

**Key:** First session should leave them thinking "I can do this" not "I'm destroyed."

### Post-Session Email (Within 24 hours)
"Great first session! You did [specific thing well]. Here's what sore muscles are normal. Next session we'll [preview]. Questions, text me anytime."

### Week 1 Check-In (Day 7)
"Hey, how are you feeling? Any soreness questions? Looking forward to seeing you on [next session date]."

### 30-Day Mini-Review (Day 30)
- Revisit initial goals
- Highlight any wins (strength gain, feeling better, completing sessions consistently)
- Set new micro-goals for the next 4 weeks
- Ask: "What's going well? What would make this even better?"

## PART 3: KEEPING CLIENTS FOR 18+ Months (Retention Multiplier)

**Key Finding from Encyclopedia:**
- Average client without retention strategy: 3–6 months
- With systematic strategies: 12–18 months
- CLV example: $75/session, 2 sessions/week for 6 months = $3,600 total → stretch to 18 months = $10,800

### Six Core Retention Strategies

**1. Regular Goal Reviews (Monthly)**
- After every 4 sessions or monthly, ask: "What progress have you noticed? What's the next goal?"
- Don't wait until they're demotivated

**2. Make Progress Visible**
- Don't just track weight/measurements
- Also track: strength (how many push-ups?), energy, sleep quality, how clothes fit, how stairs feel
- Progress without weight loss = kept clients

**3. Milestone Celebrations**
- Celebrate specific wins: "10 sessions in the books!" "First push-up!" "Lost the first 5 lbs!"
- A quick text recognizing this keeps people engaged

**4. Between-Session Check-Ins (2–3 per week)**
- Post-session: brief recap
- Mid-week: reference their specific goal ("How are the early morning walks going?")
- Pre-session: prep notes ("We're adding weight on the squats tomorrow")

**5. Community & Accountability**
- For in-home: periodic check-ins asking how they're doing outside sessions
- Referral incentive (see below)

**6. Proactive Re-Enrollment**
- 2-3 sessions BEFORE they think about stopping, bring up: "What's next?"
- Don't wait for them to leave and re-engage

### The Referral Engine — Your Best Growth Loop
**Key Finding:** Referrals + word-of-mouth is the #1 ROI channel (free–low cost, very high ROI)

**Timing:** Ask after ~session 4 (when they're committed and seeing results)

**Script:** "Who's one person you'd want to do this with?"

**Incentive:** One free session for the referrer (costs you $75, acquires a client worth $3,600+/year)

**Make it Social:** Couples/friend sessions happen organically ("My partner wants to try it"). Referrer's living room becomes the context.

## PART 4: FINANCIAL DISCIPLINE (The Unglamorous Engine)

### Monthly KPIs to Track (Take 20–30 min/month)

1. **Total Monthly Revenue** — All sessions × $75 (now) or $90 (future rate)
2. **Profit Margin** — (Revenue – Expenses) ÷ Revenue × 100. Healthy: 55–70%
3. **Active Client Count** — How many paying clients right now?
4. **Client Retention Rate** — "(Clients at end of month ÷ Clients at start of month) × 100" — Target: 80–90%
5. **Average Client Lifetime Value (CLV)** — Example: Avg client pays $75/session, 2 sessions/week, lasts 10 months = $6,000

**Minimum Viable Tracking:**
Just keep a simple spreadsheet with client names, start date, total sessions, revenue. Review monthly.

### Your Profit Margin Target
**For an in-home trainer:**
- Gas/travel: ~$200/year (minimal with tight geography)
- Insurance: ~$200/year
- CPD/certs: $220/year (NESTA GLP-1 + ACE course)
- No rent = your biggest advantage

**Example at Year 1:**
- 15 sessions/week × $75 = $1,125/week = ~$4,500/month
- Expenses: ~$50/month = $1,800/year = $150/month avg
- **Profit: ~$3,450/month = 95% margin**

This is why in-home training at per-session rates is cash gold.

### Tax Basics (US)
- Self-Employment Tax: 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare)
- Quarterly estimated payments required (April, June, Sept, Jan)
- Income tax on top (federal + state varies)
- **Deductible expenses:** Insurance, CPD, equipment, gas, phone, broadband (business portion)

Set aside ~30% of revenue for taxes to avoid surprises. Use a separate account.

### Accountant vs DIY
For an in-home solo trainer, DIY is reasonable if:
- Single income source (just training)
- Minimal expenses
- Time to learn your tax software (free tools exist)

Cost if you hire: $500–1,500/year (fully tax-deductible)
