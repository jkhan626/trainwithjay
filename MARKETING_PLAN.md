# Train With Jay — Marketing Plan
*In-home personal training · Rockaway peninsula (11694) · June 2026*

## The one-paragraph strategy
Nobody offers in-home training on the peninsula — Svetness/FlexIt/Fyt claim Queens but won't make the trip, and every local option (Orangetheory, Burn Fitness, the YMCA) makes you come to them. 11694 is affluent ($104K+ median household; Belle Harbor/Neponsit higher), full of time-starved commuters and NYPD/FDNY/teacher families who hire **"one of us" through word of mouth**, not apps. So the plan is: fill the first 10 founding slots through your warm network (the ex-wife playbook), make Google + Facebook + Nextdoor say "personal trainer Rockaway = Jay," and let referrals compound. The website exists to convert referrals and own local search — it is not the lead source. **You** are the lead source.

## The offer (locked)
- **45-minute 1:1 session at the client's home.** Jay travels; client provides dumbbells (~$50 shopping list if they have none); Jay brings bands/mat.
- **$75/session standard · $60 founding rate, first 10 clients, locked for life.** Per-session only — cash, Venmo, Zelle. No packages, no contracts.
- **Raise trigger:** when you're ~70% booked (roughly 15+ sessions/week), new clients pay $90. Existing clients keep their rate forever — that's the loyalty engine. (Research says NYC in-home runs $90–$120+; you'll grow into it. $90 is not the launch price — it's the year-one ceiling.)
- Target: gen-pop weight loss, 30–55, on or near the peninsula.
- Upsells later, not now: couples sessions (+$25–30), the $249/mo app-coaching tier for people who move away or want fewer in-person sessions.

## Unit economics (why this works)
- 10 founding clients × 1.5 sessions/week avg = **15 sessions/week = $900/week ≈ $46K/year**, before any client pays $75+.
- At $75 with 20 sessions/week: **$1,500/week ≈ $78K/year.** No rent, no revenue split, gas + insurance (~$200/yr) as the only real costs.
- 45-min sessions + tight geography (the whole peninsula is ~20 min end to end) = you can stack 3 sessions in an evening block.

## Phase 1 — Founding 10 (weeks 1–4): the ex-wife playbook
She filled a schedule at $75 on word of mouth alone. Same move:

1. **The announcement text.** Personally text 20–30 people on the peninsula (friends, neighbors, parents from school, gym buddies, the autism-walk crowd): *"Hey — I'm officially taking on personal training clients. I come to your house, 45 minutes, $60/session for my first 10 people (locks in for life). Know anyone who's been meaning to get back in shape?"* Note it asks for **referrals**, not pity bookings — people love playing matchmaker.
2. **Facebook/Instagram personal post** — same message, plus a photo and the site link. Your personal page first; it'll outperform any business page for years.
3. **First session free-ish?** No. Free devalues you. Instead: *"first session, if you don't want a second one, don't pay me."* Costs you nothing (nobody invokes it), kills all risk objections.
4. **Wife/husband doubles:** when a woman 35–50 books, the spouse often wants in. Offer the couples add-on early.
5. Goal: **10 founding clients = full.** Announce "founding rate is gone" loudly when it happens — scarcity that's actually true.

## Referral engine (the actual growth loop)
- Every client, after ~session 4: *"Who's one person you'd want to do this with?"*
- **Referral = one free session for the referrer.** (A $60 cost to acquire a client worth $3–5K/yr. Best ad you'll ever buy.)
- Couples/friend add-on makes referrals literal: they invite the friend into their own living room.

## Operating rhythm (1 hr/week of marketing, max)
- **Mon:** post GBP update + 1 group comment.
- **After each new client's session 1:** text asking how it went. After session 4: referral ask. After a milestone: review ask.
- **Monthly:** check Google Business insights (calls/clicks), count sessions/week, decide nothing until the trend is 4 weeks old.

## What NOT to do
- No paid Meta/Google ads until word-of-mouth stalls — in this neighborhood it likely won't.
- No packages, ClassPass, Thumbtack, or marketplace listings (Fyt/Svetness take 30–50% and you'd be building their brand in your own zip).
- No claiming CSCS until it's in hand; "25 years under the bar" + CPR/AED is the honest pitch. Add CSCS to the site the day you pass — and that's the $90 price-raise moment too.
- Don't mention r/fitness. Ever.
