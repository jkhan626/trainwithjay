# 1. Google Business Profile (GBP) — Service-Area Business

Google ranks local results on **relevance, distance, and popularity** ([Google](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091?hl=en)). You can't change distance, but you control relevance (categories, services, description) and popularity (reviews, posts, engagement). In 2025 Google added **"popularity"** signals: review velocity/recency, click-through to your profile, and interactions like calls and direction requests ([Service Hero](https://gowithhero.com/blog/google-business-profile-2025-changes/)).

### 1a. Set it up as a SERVICE-AREA business (critical)

- At creation, when asked "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?" → **No**. This makes it a Service-Area Business (SAB) and **hides your home address** (you train in clients' homes — never publish your home).
- **Set service areas** to your actual towns: Rockaway Beach, Rockaway Park, Belle Harbor, Neponsit, Breezy Point, Arverne, Far Rockaway, Edgemere. (Google allows up to ~20 areas; don't pad with far-off towns — keep it to where you'll actually drive.)
- **Verify** the profile. Unverified profiles are deprioritized — "verification tells Google you're authorized to represent the business, so it's more likely to show up" ([Google](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091?hl=en)).

### 1b. Categories — the single most important local-pack factor

Primary category is **the #1 local-pack ranking factor**, and businesses using **4+ additional categories rank highest** (avg map rank 5.9) ([BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)).

- **Primary category:** `Personal Trainer`
- **Additional categories (add all that apply):**
  - `Physical Fitness Program`
  - `Fitness Center` (only if it fits how you describe yourself; otherwise skip)
  - `Weight Loss Service` (strong for the GLP-1 angle)
  - `Sports Medicine Clinic` — skip (clinical, don't claim)
  - Prefer: `Personal Trainer` + `Physical Fitness Program` + `Weight Loss Service`. Add `Wellness Center` / `Health Consultant` only if Google offers them and they're honest.

> Don't keyword-stuff categories you don't do — Google can suspend SABs for misrepresentation. Pick the 3-4 that are genuinely true.

# 2. Review Generation — get to 10+ Google reviews fast

Reviews are now the **2nd-strongest local-pack factor**, and **recency + steady growth** both rank independently ([BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-local-algorithm-and-ranking-factors/)). 10 reviews with text > 30 star-only ratings.

### 2a. Get your DIRECT review link (skip the search step)

Two reliable formats:

1. **From GBP dashboard (easiest):** In your Business Profile, click **"Ask for reviews"** → Google shows your unique short link, usually `https://g.page/r/XXXXXXXX/review`. Copy it.
2. **Place ID format (bulletproof, never breaks):**
   `https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID`
   Find your Place ID at Google's **Place ID Finder** (developers.google.com/maps/documentation/places/web-service/place-id) — search your business, copy the ID, drop it into the URL above.

Then **shorten it** (bit.ly or a `rockawaytrainer.com/review` redirect) so it's textable and memorable. Test it on your phone — it should open straight to the star/write screen.

> Put `rockawaytrainer.com/review` (a Netlify redirect → your review link) on every touchpoint: email signature, text follow-ups, a small printed card you leave after sessions, a QR code.

### 2b. The exact ask flow (timing is everything)

**Ask within 24-48 hours of a great session/milestone** — the longer you wait, the lower the follow-through ([Angle180](https://www.angle180.com/insights/how-to-create-google-business-review-link)). Best moment: right after a client says something positive ("I feel so much stronger") — that's your cue.

**The two-step ask (in person, then text):**

In person, end of session:
> "Hey, quick favor — I'm a solo trainer, so Google reviews are basically how new people in Rockaway find me. If you've been happy with how it's going, would you mind leaving a quick review? I'll text you the link right now so it's one tap."

Then text immediately (while you're standing there):

### 2c. Get to 10 fast — the launch sprint

- Make a list of every current + past client, friend you've **legitimately trained** (this isn't cold-texting friends to sell — it's asking people you've actually coached for honest feedback, which is fair game).
- Ask **one or two per day**, not all at once — Google flags sudden review spikes, and steady velocity is itself a ranking factor. ~2-3 reviews/week for a few weeks looks natural and ranks better.
- **Reply to every review** within a day (replying is a ranking + trust signal per [Google](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091?hl=en)). Template: *"Thank you, [NAME] — proud of the work you're putting in. See you next session! 💪"*
- Never offer payment/discounts for reviews (against Google policy; risks removal).

# 3. Nextdoor — hyperlocal lead-gen without being spammy

Nextdoor is **the** platform for in-home services because it's neighborhood-verified. 67% of users actively share local recs ([NESTA](https://www.nestacertified.com/how-personal-trainers-and-coaches-can-benefit-from-nextdoor-com/)), and a business page with **5+ recommendations sees ~30% more engagement** ([NESTA](https://www.nestacertified.com/how-personal-trainers-and-coaches-can-benefit-from-nextdoor-com/)).

### 3a. Setup
- Claim + verify a **free Business Page**. Fill contact info, expertise, service area (Rockaway neighborhoods), and that you train **in clients' homes**.
- Get your **unique recommendation link** (you get this when you verify the page).

### 3b. The non-spammy operating rules
- **You may ask clients, friends, family, and community contacts** for recommendations — they don't have to be paying transactions ([Nextdoor](https://business.nextdoor.com/en-us/blog/how-businesses-can-get-more-recommendations-on-nextdoor)).
- **HARD RULE: never offer a reward for a recommendation** — Nextdoor will remove you ([Nextdoor](https://business.nextdoor.com/en-us/blog/how-businesses-can-get-more-recommendations-on-nextdoor)). (So your referral *cash/free-session* offers stay OFF Nextdoor.)
- **Always disclose your relationship** when you reply to a "looking for a trainer" thread and tag your business.

### 3c. The play that actually works (value-first, post every ~2 weeks)

Don't post ads. Post **helpful neighbor content**; the leads come from people DMing you ([NESTA](https://www.nestacertified.com/how-personal-trainers-and-coaches-can-benefit-from-nextdoor-com/)).

- Submit a **sitemap** (`/sitemap.xml`) and use **URL Inspection → Request Indexing** for the homepage.
- Add a `robots.txt` that allows crawling.
- **Link GBP → website** and **website → GBP** (the `sameAs` CID link above).

- Build a handful of **consistent citations** (exact same Name/Phone/area): Nextdoor, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, Facebook Page (a *business* page is fine — not your personal profile), Yellow Pages. # 5. Referral System That Compounds

Referrals are your cheapest, highest-trust channel. The winning structure is **double-sided** (rewards both parties) so the referrer isn't just doing you a favor and the new client gets a low-risk entry ([Institute of PT](https://instituteofpersonaltrainers.com/blog/the-short-guide-to-referral-marketing-for-personal-trainers); [PTDC](https://www.theptdc.com/articles/personal-training-referrals)).

### 5a. The offer (simple, double-sided)
> **"Give a free session, get a free session."**
> When a current client refers someone who books their first paid session, **the new client's intro session is free** AND **the referrer gets one free session** ($75 value each).

Why it works: the new client risks nothing, the referrer is rewarded, and one $75 give-up routinely yields a multi-month client. (Keep referral rewards **off Nextdoor** per §3b.)

### 5b. The ask scripts

**Soft, after a milestone (the PTDC approach — never awkward):**
```
"You've made real progress these last few weeks. By the way — I've got room for a couple new Rockaway clients, and I run a referral deal: if you send me someone and they book, you both get a free session. No pressure, just if anyone comes to mind."
```

**Specific prompt (works better than 'know anyone?'):**
```
"Who's the one person in your life who's been talking about getting in shape — maybe someone on a weight-loss med who wants to keep their strength? That's exactly who I help. Want me to send you something you can forward?"
```

**The forwardable message (text it to the client to pass along):**
```
Hey! My trainer [NAME] does in-home strength training here in Rockaway — he's CSCS-certified and really good, especially if you're on a GLP-1 and want to keep muscle. He's giving free intro sessions to people I refer. Want his number? (347) 671-2813 / rockawaytrainer.com
```

### 5c. Make it compound (the system, not a one-off)
- **Ask every client at their first milestone** (4-6 weeks in) — bake it into your process, not a random thought.
- **Token/tracker:** keep a simple note per client of referrals given; tell them the tally ("that's 2 friends — your next one earns you a free month idea"). Visible progress drives more referrals ([PTDC token system](https://www.theptdc.com/articles/the-token-system-for-getting-personal-trainer-referrals-and-improving-adherence)).
- **Optional quarterly contest:** "Whoever refers the most new clients by [date] gets a free month." Competition tripled clients in documented cases ([Referral Rock](https://referralrock.com/blog/personal-trainer-referral-program/)).
- **Close the loop:** when a referral books, **thank the referrer immediately** and deliver their free session fast. Fast reward = more referrals.

# 6. Lead Capture & Conversion (Netlify Forms)

Right now text/call/email only — inquiries that don't call **leak**. Add a dead-simple form. Median form conversion is ~4.7%; top forms hit 11%+, and **fewer fields + email-first** lift completion ([LeadSquared](https://www.leadsquared.com/learn/marketing/lead-capture-forms/), [LeadsHook](https://www.leadshook.com/blog/lead-capture-forms-best-practices/)).

### 6a. The form (drop into your HTML — Netlify auto-detects it)

Keep it to **4 fields max**. Email/phone first.

```html
<form name="intro" method="POST" data-netlify="true" netlify-honeypot="bot-field" action="/thanks.html">
  <input type="hidden" name="form-name" value="intro" />
  <p hidden><label>Don't fill this: <input name="bot-field" /></label></p>

  <label>Name<input type="text" name="name" required /></label>
  <label>Phone or email<input type="text" name="contact" required placeholder="Best way to reach you" /></label>
  <label>Which part of Rockaway?<input type="text" name="area" placeholder="e.g. Belle Harbor" /></label>
  <label>What's your goal? (optional)
    <textarea name="goal" rows="2" placeholder="e.g. keep muscle on Wegovy"></textarea>
  </label>

  <button type="submit">Book my free intro session</button>
</form>
```

- `data-netlify="true"` + the hidden `form-name` = Netlify captures it, no backend ([Netlify Docs](https://docs.netlify.com/manage/forms/setup/)).
- `netlify-honeypot="bot-field"` blocks spam without a CAPTCHA (CAPTCHAs kill conversion).
- `action="/thanks.html"` → a thank-you page that resets expectations ("I'll text you within a few hours").

### 6b. Instant notification (speed-to-lead is everything)
Set a **Netlify form notification** to email you on every submission, and ideally **SMS** (via a Netlify → Zapier/email-to-SMS hook) so you reply within **5-15 minutes** ([Netlify Docs](https://docs.netlify.com/manage/forms/notifications/)). Fast response is the #1 conversion lever for inbound leads.

### 6c. CTA + microcopy that converts
- **Button copy:** "Book my free intro session" (outcome-focused) beats "Submit."
- **Reduce risk** right next to the form: *"Free 20-min consult. No gym, no contract. I come to you. $75/session after."*
- **One primary CTA**, repeated: tap-to-call at top, form in the middle, call/text/email in footer. Don't make them hunt.
- Keep the **tel: link** prominent — many will still just call, and that's fine.

### 6d. The reply that books the session (your speed-to-lead text)
```
Hi [NAME], it's Jamal Khan from Rockaway Trainer — thanks for reaching out! I'd love to set up your free 20-min intro. I've got [DAY] at [TIME] or [DAY] at [TIME] open — which works? (And what part of Rockaway are you in?)
```
