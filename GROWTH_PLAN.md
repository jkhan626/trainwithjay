## Research-backed do's and don'ts (so you don't waste effort)

**Worth it:**
- A **small, tightly-targeted Google Search ad** on "Ozempic trainer near me" / "in-home personal trainer [neighborhood]" as a *bridge* while SEO matures. High-intent. (Different from broad Facebook awareness ads.)
- A **free 20-minute intro consult** (a sales call, not a free workout) as your low-risk entry offer.
- **Pairing two clients into semi-private** sessions — raises perceived value and your $/hour, seeds word-of-mouth.
- **Corporate-wellness invoices** — many NYC in-home clients can claim reimbursement; offering a formatted invoice is an easy differentiator.

**Waste of time (per real trainers):**
- **Broad paid Facebook/Google display ads** for $75 1:1 training — you only need a handful of clients; mass reach mismatches the product.
- **Fully free sessions** — attract the wrong people and devalue you. (Your "first session, don't pay if you don't want a second" is fine; a *free consult* is fine. A free *workout* to strangers is not.)
- **"Look at me" self-promotion** — content about you converts worse than content about the client's problem. Lead with "I come to you" and the GLP-1 muscle problem, not your résumé.
