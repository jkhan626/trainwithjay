Training on a GLP-1

The medication takes care of your appetite. It doesn't decide what kind of weight comes off. Without strength training and enough protein, studies show a third or more of what you lose can be muscle, and muscle is the part that keeps you strong, keeps your metabolism up, and is slow to build back.

  • Strength training once or twice a week, at your home
  • Protein habits that work with a smaller appetite
  • Every session logged, so you can see your strength holding while the scale drops

I'm not a doctor. Anything about the medication itself stays between you and your prescriber. I handle the training.

Questions I get a lot

I'm really out of shape. Is this going to be embarrassing?

No. Most of my clients start exactly there, and there's nobody else around: no mirrors, no audience. The first session is mostly me watching how you move so I can build your plan. We start where you are.

I'm on Ozempic, Wegovy, or Zepbound. Is training different for me?

Yes, in two ways that matter. The appetite suppression makes it easy to under-eat protein without noticing, and fast weight loss without lifting takes muscle down with the fat. So we strength train once or twice a week and keep your protein honest. The goal is to arrive at your target weight strong, not just smaller. Anything about the medication itself is between you and your doctor.

What equipment do I need?

A pair of dumbbells and enough floor space to lie down. That covers months of progress. If you don't own anything, I'll text you a shopping list before we start, about $50 total. You buy once and keep it. I bring bands and a mat.

Why per-session instead of packages?

Packages lock you in before you know whether this is working. I'd rather you book the second session because the first one was worth it. You pay as we go (cash, Venmo, or Zelle) and you can stop anytime.

How often should I train with you?

Once or twice a week for most people. I'll also set you up with simple workouts to do on your own between sessions, logged in the app so I can see them and adjust your plan.

Is 45 minutes really enough?

Yes. There's no waiting for machines and no commute on your end. We warm up, do the work, and you're done before you would have found parking at a gym.

Do you train couples?

Usually, yes. If you and a spouse or a friend want to share a session, text me and we'll figure it out.
